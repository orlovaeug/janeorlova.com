An empirical analysis of how physical appearance cues shape perceptions of trustworthiness, competence, and leadership suitability in Dutch national politics — with quantitative findings across demographic and cultural subgroups.

Political Communication Research Survey-Experimental Design Dutch Political Context
§ 1
Overview & Key Findings

First Impressions Shape
Political Judgements

This survey-experimental study examined the degree to which physical appearance influences political perception across five dimensions: physical attractiveness, trustworthiness, competence, approachability, and perceived leadership potential. Respondents rated three Dutch political figures presented as Persons A, B, and C — corresponding to Rob Jetten, Thierry Baudet, and Dick Schoof — without explicit identification cues in the instrument design.

The results align with a substantial body of political psychology literature suggesting that superficial appearance cues — processed rapidly and often unconsciously — exert measurable influence on voters' assessments of political suitability. Our findings document a consistent perceptual hierarchy across all five measured dimensions, with meaningful variation by political engagement level, European cultural identity, and gender.

Principal Finding
Across all measured dimensions, perceived competence is the strongest single predictor of leadership suitability (Jetten r = .791; Baudet r = .644; Schoof r = .710). Physical appearance, while significant, operates primarily as an indirect cue — filtering initial impressions that are subsequently refined via trustworthiness and approachability assessments. Appearance is where perception begins; competence is where it lands.

§ 3
Profession Attribution

What Profession Do
They Look Like?

Beyond numerical ratings, respondents answered an open-text question: "What profession do you think this person most likely has?" These free associations provide a window into the implicit mental models respondents apply when decoding political faces — and carry significant implications for political communication strategy, visual branding, and voter perception research. Responses were coded into seven profession categories by independent reviewers.

The distribution reveals meaningfully different appearance archetypes for each politician. Critically, the diversity of guesses is itself a finding: a wide spread across categories indicates an appearance that resists coherent archetype formation, which generally suppresses leadership perception among low-information audiences.

Profession Attribution — Strategic Insight
Jetten's appearance activates a dual signal — political authority (30%) combined with corporate and technical competence (Business Executive 21%, Finance 15%, Tech 15%). This is the most coherent archetype in the study: his look reads as institutionally capable regardless of whether the viewer knows his political role.

Baudet's profile is the most elite-but-ambiguous: Finance and Legal together account for 39% of attributions, alongside notable Actor and Architecture mentions. His appearance communicates high-status formation without warmth or institutional accountability.

Schoof generates the most heterogeneous free-associations — including "Petty Theft," "Car salesman," and "Retired physician" alongside legitimate political attributions. While he has the highest politician recognition rate (34%), this recognition did not translate into leadership preference.
"Jetten reads as executive; Baudet as elite-ambiguous; Schoof as bureaucratic. Each archetype carries its own ceiling for public leadership perception."

Communication Implication
For political communication professionals, the data suggest that projecting competence through visual and rhetorical cues should be the primary objective in image strategy — not physical attractiveness per se. Approachability shows consistent mid-range correlations (r = .508–.554) across all three politicians, indicating it acts as a moderating variable that converts perceived competence into actual leadership trust.

Strategic Insight · Political Engagement
The most politically engaged respondents show the largest gap favouring Jetten (Δ = 2.43 pts vs Baudet). Among those with no political engagement, Baudet leads — suggesting his appearance generates stronger generic leadership cues when respondents lack partisan context.

§ 9
Limitations & Confounds

Methodological Considerations

Recognition bias. A significant proportion of European respondents correctly identified one or more politicians within the open-text profession field. This contamination cannot be disentangled from appearance-based impressions.

Sample composition. The sample skews young (predominantly 18–34) and female, which limits generalisability to the broader Dutch electorate.

Common method variance. All five perceptual dimensions were collected from the same respondent in the same session, meaning dimension–leadership correlations are likely inflated relative to a multi-source or longitudinal design.

§ 10
Conclusions

Conclusions for Political
Communication Practice

This study confirms that physical appearance significantly shapes political perception — but operates through a structured hierarchy of inferential cues. Appearance is an entry point, not a destination: it primes assessments of competence, trustworthiness, and approachability, which collectively determine whether a leader impression crystallises into genuine preference.

Strategic Recommendation
Political image management should prioritise the triad of competence projection, trustworthiness signalling, and approachability cues — in that order of explanatory power. Any visual communication strategy that optimises appearance at the expense of warm, credible, accessible demeanour is likely to produce Baudet's pattern: high perceived ability, low trust, suppressed leadership preference.
Methodology: Survey-experimental design. Respondents rated three Dutch politicians across five perceptual dimensions (1–10 scale). Open-text profession attributions coded into seven categories. Paired-sample t-tests used for significance testing. Pearson r reported for dimension–leadership correlations. Significance thresholds: * p < .05, *** p < .001 (two-tailed, estimated df ≈ 42).

Analytical Note

De werkelijke emotielexicon-analyse van Nederlandstalige bronnen (23 februari – 6 april 2026) laat een genuanceerder beeld zien dan verwacht. Vrees domineert alleen bij de PVV-oppositie (58%) en is vrijwel afwezig in de coalitietaal. Het kabinet-Jetten en minister Van den Brink hanteren overwegend vertrouwenstaal — procedures, wetgeving, uitvoering, akkoord — met gemiddeld 44% (coalitieakkoord) en 74% (IND/EU Pact). Hoop domineert juist bij de oppositie van GL-PvdA (77%) en VluchtelingenWerk (54%), die inzetten op integratie, meedoen en toekomstperspectief.

De analyse onthult een structurele emotionele polarisatie: de coalitie beheerst het discours via procedureel vertrouwen, de PVV via angst, en de progressieve oppositie en NGOs via hoop. Woede is slechts significant bij de PVV (42%) en mediaframing (25%). De media (NOS/NRC) toont het meest gemengde profiel — fear 29%, anger 25%, hope 31% — wat de gefragmenteerde aard van het debat weerspiegelt. Dominante emotie per actor: vertrouwen (coalitie, IND), vrees (PVV), hoop (GL-PvdA, NGO).

§ M
Methodology

Scores zijn berekend door Nederlandstalige tekstcorpora per segment samen te stellen uit echte publieke bronnen (NOS.nl, Tweede Kamer verslagen, VluchtelingenWerk.nl, GroenLinks-PvdA.nl, coalitieakkoord D66/VVD/CDA, rijksoverheid.nl) en te scoren tegen een Nederlands emotielexicon gebaseerd op de NRC Word-Emotion Association Lexicon (Mohammad & Turney, 2013). Emotiecategorieën: vrees [crisis, bedreiging, instroom, onveiligheid, chaos…], woede [falen, onverantwoordelijk, schandalig, bezuinigingen…], hoop [toekomst, integratie, meedoen, samenwerking…], vertrouwen [procedure, wetgeving, akkoord, stabiliteit…]. Tokens worden genormaliseerd naar 100% over de vier categorieën per segment. Steekproeven kleiner dan 30 tokens zijn gemarkeerd.

emotion_map_nl.py haalt live paginatekst op, tokeniseert, verwijdert stopwoorden en berekent trefferfrequenties. Het script schrijft emotion_results.json; GitHub Actions voert het wekelijks uit zodat de scores automatisch worden bijgewerkt.

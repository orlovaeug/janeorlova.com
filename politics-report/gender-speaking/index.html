The 2025 elections transformed the Tweede Kamer's composition. PVV lost 11 seats (37→26); D66 gained 17 (9→26). NSC, which held 20 seats in 2023, was entirely wiped out. CDA surged from 5 to 18. The result: a Kamer with a markedly different gender geography, because the parties that grew — D66, CDA — had much higher female representation than the parties that dominated in 2023.

65 women were installed on 12 November 2025 — a record. They come from 12 of 15 parties. Only SGP (3 seats), DENK (3 seats), and Volt (1 seat, sole MP) have zero women. Compared to 2023 — when four parties sent all-male delegations including FvD, JA21, DENK, and SGP — this represents structural progress.

The key structural change is the collapse of PVV's dominance. In the 2023 term, PVV's 37 seats and 19% female share structurally depressed Kamer-wide speaking averages, particularly in the asylum and migration debates that dominated 2024-25. With PVV now at 26 seats (and effectively 19 after the Groep-Markuszower split in January 2026), this structural drag is reduced.

D66's rise is the most significant gender story of 2025. From 9 seats (5 women, 56%) to 26 seats (13 women, 50%) — it is now both the joint-largest party and a majority-female delegation. With Rob Jetten as PM and D66 anchoring the Jetten cabinet, the party that leads the government is also one with near-parity internal composition. Whether this translates to speaking time parity is the question the 2025 Handelingen data will answer.

The most significant gender story of 2025 is compositional, not numerical: GL-PvdA's female share rose to 70% even as the party lost 5 seats. VVD's female share fell from 54% to 32% — a sharp reversal. The CDA surge (+13 seats, 50% female) is the largest positive contribution to the 2025 record. NSC's elimination removed 8 female MPs from the equation but also 12 males; its disappearance net-improved the Kamer's gender balance.

03
Speaking Time & Topics
Seat share moved.
Speaking patterns are slower to shift.

The 2023-term Handelingen analysis documented a consistent topic segregation: women spoke above their 39.3% seat share in health, education, and social affairs, and well below it in defence, foreign affairs, and asylum. The asylum debate was the most imbalanced: PVV's 81% male delegation drove floor time in the politically dominant topic of 2024-25.

The 2025 term (from November 2025) is still early — full Handelingen analysis is forthcoming. But the structural conditions for change are present. The dominant topic has shifted: asylum has given way to European defence, housing, and climate. The parties most associated with those topics (D66, GL-PvdA, CDA) have higher female representation. The compositional engine driving speaking segregation in 2023 is smaller.

Two early signals from 2025-term plenary records (November 2025 – April 2026) suggest the gap is narrowing. In defence debates — historically the most male-dominated — Dilan Yeşilgöz (VVD) as minister and the cabinet's European defence focus have elevated female voices on a topic that was previously male-dominated by PVV and Schoof-era ministers. In housing debates, the topic remains female-led but now with more prominence.

The topic segregation hypothesis for 2025: women will continue to lead on health, education, and social affairs. The question is whether European defence — now the politically central topic under Jetten — follows the pattern of the 2023 asylum debates (male-dominated) or begins to shift as the minister herself is female. The Handelingen data will answer this in the coming months.

The 2023 Kamer stalled at 39.3% — identical to 2021 — because PVV's 37-seat surge (19% female) offset the near-parity delegations of progressive parties. It was, as Stem op een Vrouw noted, "een klein wonder" the percentage didn't fall.

2025 breaks the pattern because the seat gains went to parties with higher female shares: D66 (+17, 50% female) and CDA (+13, 50% female) together added 30 seats, of which approximately 15 are women. PVV's loss of 11 seats removed 2 female MPs but 9 males — net positive for female share.

Three parties still send all-male delegations: SGP (3 seats, on religious grounds), DENK (3 seats), and Volt — now a one-person faction with a male MP. Together they hold 7 seats, compared to 10 in 2023. Progress, but not elimination.

The gender composition of the Jetten cabinet (36% female, with Yeşilgöz at Defence) reflects a similar pattern: formal progress, persistent gaps in high-profile portfolios. The research continues tracking whether the 2025 Kamer's record composition translates into proportional floor time — the metric that actually measures political voice.

01
Language Diffusion
PVV sets the terms.
The mainstream follows — then loses power.

The dominant pattern across nine years is a consistent fringe-to-mainstream flow of political vocabulary. Terms originating in PVV press releases appear in VVD and CDA communications with a 12–24 month lag. The clearest case: asielstop — first used in PVV manifestos in 2017, reached NSC and VVD by 2023, and entered the Schoof coalition agreement ("Hoop, Lef en Trots," May 2024) as governing policy language.

The reverse flow — from left-progressive parties — operates equally on social and climate topics. Bestaanszekerheid (economic security) was a PvdA frame from the 2020-21 cycle, adopted by D66, VVD, and CDA by 2021, and became the defining phrase of that election. Klimaatcrisis (crisis framing, not klimaatverandering) was a Volt/GL frame from 2019 that reached the Jetten coalition agreement in January 2026.

The 2025 elections mark a language regime change. With PVV shrinking from 37 to 26 seats and D66 surging to joint-largest, the vocabulary that dominates plenary debate is shifting. Asylum and migration — the PVV-anchored topics of 2024-25 — are being displaced by European defence, climate investment, and housing. The parties that grew in 2025 (D66, CDA) are also the parties whose vocabulary is gaining floor time.

This creates a natural experiment: will the Jetten cabinet's coalition agreement language ("Aan de slag," January 2026) — which centres on Europese defensie, woningbouw, and climate framing — now diffuse back from coalition language into opposition party communications? Early 2026 press release analysis suggests PVV has already adopted Europese defensie as an opposition frame — but to oppose, not endorse. The semantic direction of adoption matters as much as its occurrence.

03
Corpus Similarity
Coalition makes parties sound alike.
Divergence predicts collapse.

TF-IDF cosine similarity tracks how similar party press release corpora are over time. The clearest finding: parties governing together adopt more similar vocabulary, and vocabulary divergence appears to precede political divergence. In the months before PVV left the Schoof coalition in June 2025, PVV–VVD cosine similarity on defence/Ukraine terms dropped sharply — while migration-term similarity held. Language divergence preceded the political crisis by approximately 6–8 weeks.

The 2025 elections produced a new similarity landscape. D66 (26 seats) and GL-PvdA (20 seats) — both with high historical vocabulary similarity on EU, climate, and housing — now dominate the progressive-centre space. Their press release corpora have converged further since the Jetten coalition formed, as they share governing agreement language.

The most striking distinctiveness finding of 2025: NSC's elimination from the Kamer removed the party with the fastest-converging vocabulary in the corpus. Between 2022 and 2025, NSC's language had moved from a distinctive good-governance/rule-of-law framing toward generic centre-right vocabulary — a linguistic sign of a party that lost its distinctive identity before losing its voters.

BBB shows the same pattern at a slower pace: its highly distinctive agrarian vocabulary of 2022 (nitrogen, farmers, rural identity) has been absorbed into generic right-populist framing by 2024-25. After Mona Keijzer left BBB in February 2026, the party's press release vocabulary shifted again — a small but detectable change the corpus captures within weeks.

04
2025 Language Landscape
New government,
new frame competition.

What changed in 2025: NSC's elimination removed the party with the most generic convergent vocabulary. PVV's seat loss reduced the dominance of migration framing in Kamer debate. D66's surge elevated European, climate, and progressive housing vocabulary. CDA's revival brought a distinctive Christian-democratic social framing back into the mainstream.

The Jetten coalition agreement "Aan de slag" (January 2026) introduced three new dominant frames: Europese defensie, woningbouw versnelling, and eigen risico (in healthcare). Of these, the first is genuinely new in coalition language — the second and third are recycled from earlier cycles but now with governing weight.

The new opposition vocabulary competition: With PVV, GL-PvdA, JA21, FvD, and SP all in opposition, the right-wing opposition (PVV+JA21+FvD = 42 seats) and left opposition (GL-PvdA+SP+PvdD = 26 seats) are now developing opposing frames around the Jetten government's language. PVV has already begun framing Europese defensie as capitulating to Brussels — adopting the term to oppose it.

The 2025 corpus will be the most analytically rich in the dataset. A coalition that formed after a right-wing collapse, with a PM who moved his party rightward to win, governing with two parties whose language it partly adopted — the conditions for rapid vocabulary diffusion and counter-framing are all present. Full corpus analysis of the 2025-26 period is forthcoming.

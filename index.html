Understanding the choice

I'm Jane Orlova — a political researcher and writer based in the Netherlands since 2018. My background spans International Relations (Moscow State University) and Political Communication (MSc, University of Amsterdam), with research conducted at the University of Bologna during my Erasmus exchange.

My thesis examined how news framing shapes public support for climate refugees — and how emotions and political knowledge modify that effect. That question still sits at the centre of everything I find compelling: why people believe what they believe, how messaging moves public opinion, and who controls the frame.

I write about Dutch and European politics — elections, party strategy, political leadership, and the mechanics of how public opinion actually moves. I am particularly drawn to campaigns: how they are built, where they win people over, and where they lose them.
